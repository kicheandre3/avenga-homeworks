Atomic weight:

40.078

Calcium (Ca) is an alkaline earth metal with atomic number 20, known for its role in biological systems and its abundance in the Earth's crust. It is a soft, silvery-white metal that is highly reactive, tarnishing quickly in air and reacting with water to form calcium hydroxide and hydrogen gas. Essential for life, calcium is crucial for bone and teeth health, muscle contraction, and blood clotting, and is found in minerals like limestone, gypsum, and fluorite.
