Br
Bromine

Atomic number:

35

Atomic weight:

79.904

The Br element is the chemical symbol for bromine, a reddish-brown, volatile, nonmetallic liquid at room temperature. As a halogen, it is in group 17 of the periodic table and has an atomic number of 35. Bromine's properties make it useful in various industries, including flame retardants, water treatment, and pharmaceuticals, although it is highly corrosive and toxic, requiring careful handling.
