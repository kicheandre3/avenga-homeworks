Sodium (Na), an alkali metal with atomic number 11, is a soft, silvery-white, highly reactive element found abundantly in nature, primarily as the compound sodium chloride (salt). It is essential for biological functions but is too reactive to exist in its pure form in the environment, reacting vigorously with water to produce hydrogen gas and sodium hydroxide. Its high reactivity makes it useful for various industrial applications, and it is a vital nutrient for humans.
