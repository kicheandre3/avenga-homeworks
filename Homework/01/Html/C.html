Carbon

Atomic number:

6

Atomic weight:

12.01

Carbon (C) is a nonmetal with atomic number 6 and an atomic weight of about 12.01. It is tetravalent, forming up to four covalent bonds, which makes it extremely versatile in building molecules.

Carbon's allotropes include diamond and graphite; it is the foundation of organic chemistry and essential to all known life, appearing in fuels, polymers, and many advanced materials.
