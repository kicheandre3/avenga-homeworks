Iron (Fe) is a highly abundant and versatile metallic element with atomic number 26, known for its strength and vital role in both industrial and biological applications. It is a transition metal found in Earth's crust and core, commonly sourced from ores like hematite and magnetite, and is used to make steel, which is essential for everything from construction to small tools.
