Titanium

Atomic number:

22

Atomic weight:

47.88

Titanium (Ti) is a lustrous, silver-colored transition metal with atomic number 22, known for its high strength-to-weight ratio and excellent corrosion resistance. It is found in nature as an oxide and is the ninth most abundant element in Earth's crust. Key applications include aerospace, medical implants, and as an alloy in steel, while its compound, titanium dioxide, is used in pigments like paint and sunscreen.
