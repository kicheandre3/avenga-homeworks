Mn
Manganese

Atomic number:

25

Atomic weight:

54.938

Manganese (Mn) is a gray-white, hard, and brittle metal with atomic number 25 that is not found in its free metallic form in nature but is abundant in the Earth's crust, typically as oxides, carbonates, and silicates. It is crucial for the steel industry and has vital biological functions in plants and animals. Key properties include a high melting point of 1246°C and an electronic configuration of [Ar] 3d54s2.
